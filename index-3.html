About mutual fund overlap, portfolio diversification, and this tool.

Mutual fund overlap refers to the percentage of common stocks held across two or more mutual funds in your portfolio. High overlap means your funds invest in many of the same companies, reducing actual diversification despite holding multiple funds. For example, if both your Large Cap Fund and Flexi Cap Fund hold Reliance, HDFC Bank, and Infosys in large proportions, your effective diversification is much lower than it appears.
This mutual fund overlap calculator identifies common stocks between two funds. For each common stock, it takes the minimum weight between the two funds and sums them up. The result is the overlap percentage. For example, if Fund A holds Reliance at 8% and Fund B holds Reliance at 5%, that contributes 5% to the overlap. This method prevents double-counting and gives a realistic measure of concentration.
Generally: 0–30% overlap is considered Low — excellent diversification. 30–60% is Moderate — acceptable but monitor closely. 60%+ is High — you are essentially holding the same portfolio twice, which defeats the purpose of having multiple funds. For Indian large-cap or index funds, some overlap is expected as they all tend to hold Nifty 50 or Sensex stocks. The key is to ensure your mid-cap, small-cap, and thematic funds have low overlap with your core large-cap holdings.
Yes. This portfolio overlap calculator works for any equity mutual fund — Large Cap, Mid Cap, Small Cap, Flexi Cap, Multi Cap, ELSS, Sector Funds, Thematic Funds, and Index Funds. You can find the latest holdings (portfolio disclosure) for any Indian mutual fund on: the fund house's official website (e.g., HDFC AMC, SBI MF), AMFI India (amfiindia.com), Value Research Online (valueresearchonline.com), Morningstar India (morningstar.in), or Moneycontrol.
